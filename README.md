# European Glass Eel Recruitment: A Quantitative Research Archive

## Overview

This repository documents the quantitative research conducted during my Master's thesis on the European glass eel (*Anguilla anguilla*).

The objective of the project was to understand the large-scale dynamics of European glass eel recruitment by combining heterogeneous monitoring programs, advanced statistical modelling, and environmental time-series analyses.

Rather than relying on individual recruitment series, the research progressively developed an integrated analytical framework to:

- characterize common recruitment dynamics across Europe;
- estimate regional recruitment using a hierarchical Bayesian model;
- investigate the environmental processes potentially driving recruitment variability.

This repository preserves both the original analyses and the scientific reasoning behind their development.

---

# Research Objectives

The project addressed three complementary questions.

### 1. Can common patterns of recruitment variability be identified across Europe?

European recruitment monitoring programs differ substantially in methodology, temporal coverage, and geographical distribution.

Dynamic Factor Analysis (DFA) was used to identify shared temporal dynamics and define coherent recruitment regions.

---

### 2. Can heterogeneous recruitment observations be integrated into robust regional estimates?

The Glass Eel Recruitment Estimation Model (GEREM) was developed to combine multiple observation types within a hierarchical Bayesian framework.

GEREM integrates monitoring programs while accounting for differences in observation methods and uncertainty, producing annual recruitment estimates at regional and continental scales.

---

### 3. Which environmental processes may influence recruitment?

Using the regional recruitment estimates generated by GEREM, environmental correlation analyses were conducted to investigate whether recruitment variability could be explained by large-scale environmental conditions.

Both long-term and short-term environmental variability were examined using complementary time-series techniques.

# Repository Philosophy

This repository is intended as a **research archive** rather than a software package.

The original R scripts have been preserved in (or very close to) their historical form to maintain reproducibility and accurately reflect the work as it was conducted during the Master's project.

Rather than rewriting the code using modern programming practices, the focus has been on documenting:

- the scientific questions;
- the analytical workflow;
- the statistical methodology;
- the assumptions;
- the interpretation of the results.

The principal value of this repository therefore lies in the quantitative methods and scientific reasoning rather than software engineering.

---

# Historical Context

This work was conducted during my Master's research (2015–2016).

Although the code reflects the programming practices of that period, the accompanying documentation has been substantially expanded to explain the analytical framework from a modern perspective.

The documentation was prepared approximately ten years after completion of the original research with the objective of preserving both the methodology and the scientific reasoning for future reference.
