# Environmental Drivers of European Glass Eel Recruitment

## Overview

This repository documents the environmental correlation analyses conducted as part of my Master's research on the European eel (*Anguilla anguilla*).

The objective was to investigate whether large-scale environmental variability could explain regional recruitment dynamics observed across Europe.

Unlike many previous studies that relied on individual monitoring stations, these analyses use regional recruitment estimates produced by the **Glass Eel Recruitment Estimation Model (GEREM)**. These estimates integrate multiple monitoring programs and observation types, providing a more robust representation of large-scale recruitment dynamics.

The analyses investigate both **long-term environmental trends** and **short-term environmental variability**, using complementary time-series methods specifically designed for each temporal scale.

---

# Research Questions

The repository addresses two complementary questions.

### Long-term variability

> Are gradual environmental changes associated with the long-term decline in European eel recruitment?

Examples include:

- Gulf Stream variability
- Sea surface temperature
- Long-term climatic variability

---

### Short-term variability

> Do annual environmental anomalies explain year-to-year recruitment variability?

Examples include:

- North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO)
- Primary Production (PP)
- Gulf Stream Index (GSI)

---

# Analytical Framework

This repository represents the final stage of the analytical framework developed during my Master's thesis.

```text
European recruitment monitoring programs
                    │
                    ▼
       Dynamic Factor Analysis (DFA)
                    │
 Identification of common recruitment patterns
                    │
                    ▼
                GEREM
Hierarchical Bayesian recruitment estimation
                    │
 Regional recruitment estimates
                    │
                    ▼
 Environmental correlation analyses
```

The three components are archived separately:

- **DFA** — Identification of common recruitment dynamics across Europe.
- **GEREM** — Bayesian estimation of regional recruitment.
- **Correlation Analysis** *(this repository)* — Investigation of environmental drivers.

---

# Statistical Methods

The analyses combine several classical time-series techniques.

## Long-term analyses

- Log transformation of recruitment estimates
- Rescaling of variables
- Five-year moving averages
- Pearson correlation
- Correction for temporal autocorrelation

---

## Short-term analyses

- Trend assessment
- Stationarity evaluation
- ARIMA modelling
- Pre-whitening
- Cross-correlation analysis (CCF)
- Lag analysis

Together, these complementary approaches investigate recruitment dynamics at different temporal scales.
