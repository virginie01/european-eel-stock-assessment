# Data Sources

## Purpose

This document describes the datasets used in the environmental correlation analyses.

Unlike the previous components of the research project, the analyses presented in this repository combine two fundamentally different classes of data:

- **Regional recruitment estimates**, produced by the Glass Eel Recruitment Estimation Model (GEREM), which serve as the response variables.
- **Large-scale environmental time series**, which serve as explanatory variables representing different ecological hypotheses.

Together, these datasets were used to investigate potential environmental drivers of European glass eel (*Anguilla anguilla*) recruitment.

---

# Overview

The environmental correlation analyses are based on two categories of time series:

| Dataset | Role |
|---------|------|
| GEREM regional recruitment estimates | Response variables |
| Environmental variables | Predictor variables |

Unlike many previous studies, recruitment observations from individual rivers were not analysed directly.

Instead, all recruitment analyses were performed using the regional recruitment estimates generated by GEREM.

This approach reduces the influence of local variability, differences in monitoring methodology, and observation error.

---

# Recruitment Data

## Source

Regional recruitment estimates were obtained from the Glass Eel Recruitment Estimation Model (GEREM).

GEREM integrates heterogeneous recruitment observations from multiple European monitoring programs into annual recruitment estimates for six recruitment regions.

These regional estimates represent the best available reconstruction of annual recruitment variability at the regional scale.

---

## Recruitment Regions

The analyses were performed independently for the six recruitment regions defined within GEREM:

| Region |
|--------|
| North Sea (NS) |
| British Isles (BI) |
| English Channel (Manche) |
| French Atlantic (ATL_F) |
| Iberian Atlantic (ATL_IB) |
| Mediterranean (Med) |

These regions were previously identified through Dynamic Factor Analysis (DFA) and subsequently incorporated into the hierarchical Bayesian structure of GEREM.

# Environmental Variables

Several environmental variables representing different ecological processes were investigated.

---

## Gulf Stream Index (GSI)

### Ecological hypothesis

Variability in the Gulf Stream influences larval transport between the Sargasso Sea and Europe.

### Temporal scale

Long-term and short-term analyses.

---

## North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO)

### Ecological hypothesis

Large-scale atmospheric variability influences ocean circulation and environmental conditions experienced by eel larvae.

### Temporal scale

Primarily short-term analyses.

---

## Sea Surface Temperature (SST)

### Ecological hypothesis

Temperature influences larval development, metabolism, survival, and ocean productivity.

### Temporal scale

Primarily long-term analyses.

---

## Primary Production (PP)

### Ecological hypothesis

Primary production in the Sargasso Sea provides a proxy for food availability during the early larval stage.

### Temporal scale

Short-term analyses.

---

# Data Preparation

Recruitment estimates and environmental variables undergo several preprocessing steps before analysis.

Depending on the analytical objective, preprocessing may include:

- logarithmic transformation,
- standardization,
- moving-average smoothing,
- differencing,
- ARIMA modelling,
- pre-whitening.

Different preprocessing strategies are applied for long-term and short-term analyses.

---

# Long-Term Analyses

Long-term analyses use smoothed recruitment and environmental series to investigate gradual temporal changes.

Input datasets:

- GEREM regional recruitment estimates
- Gulf Stream Index
- Sea Surface Temperature
- (other long-term environmental variables where appropriate)

These analyses investigate whether gradual environmental changes correspond to long-term recruitment decline.

---

# Short-Term Analyses

Short-term analyses focus on interannual variability.

Input datasets:

- GEREM regional recruitment estimates
- North Atlantic Oscillation
- Primary Production
- Gulf Stream Index (where applicable)

Recruitment and environmental series are first pre-whitened using ARIMA models before cross-correlation analyses are performed.

---

# Relationship Between Datasets

The analytical framework can be summarized as:

```text
European recruitment monitoring network
                 │
                 ▼
              GEREM
                 │
Regional recruitment estimates
                 │
                 ▼
Environmental correlation analyses
                 ▲
                 │
Large-scale environmental variables
```

GEREM therefore acts as the interface between recruitment monitoring data and environmental analyses.

---

# Data Quality

The environmental analyses rely on two key assumptions regarding data quality:

1. The regional recruitment estimates accurately summarize large-scale recruitment dynamics.

2. The environmental datasets adequately represent the ecological processes hypothesized to influence recruitment.

Any remaining uncertainty in either dataset should be considered when interpreting the resulting correlations.
